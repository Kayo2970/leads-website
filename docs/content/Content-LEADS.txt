Where Leaders Are Built, Not Born
Full About Page Copy
Three years ago, LEADS Next Gen Centre didn't exist. What existed was E-Cell FMC RUAS—an entrepreneurship club focused on startups and business ideas. But as we worked with our students, we realized something: the challenges they face go far beyond entrepreneurship. They're asking how to be ethical leaders in a climate crisis. How to build teams across difference. How to use innovation for purpose, not just profit. That realization became a transformation.
E-Cell evolved into LEADS. We kept the entrepreneurial spirit—the audacity to imagine and build—but expanded our mission to develop leaders for the complex problems India actually faces. Today, LEADS is a movement across all disciplines, all batches, powered by mentors from McKinsey, World Resources Institute, and Tata Sustainability Group.
We measure success differently now. It's not about how many startups students launch. It's about the leaders they become—grounded in purpose, equipped with skills, and connected to a network of changemakers.
Vision
"To pioneer a global culture of transformative leadership by empowering students to cultivate purpose-driven attitudes, champion sustainable innovation, and lead with integrity."
Mission
"To empower future leaders through experiential learning, ethical mentorship, and sustainability-driven action."
What LEADS Does
We run eight core programs—from Innovation Challenges to Mentorship Circles to our flagship Bharat Lead Summit. Each one is designed around the principle of learning-by-doing. You don't sit in lectures about leadership. You lead. You collaborate. You face real problems and real feedback.
Our eight programs together create a scaffolded journey: whether you're a first-year trainee or a graduating core executive, there's a place for you to grow.
Governance Council
LEADS operates under a clear governance structure ensuring both academic rigor and student ownership:
• Patron: Vice-Chancellor, MSRUAS – provides institutional vision and support
• Chief Advisor: Dean, Faculty of Management & Commerce – oversees strategic alignment
• Centre Head: Nominated faculty member – manages day-to-day operations and mentorship
• Student Leadership: President, General Secretary, Vice-President, and Chief Coordinator lead execution alongside Core Executives and Trainee Associates.
Our Team
Our strength is in our structure. First-year Trainee Associates bring energy and fresh perspectives. Core Executives (second-year and beyond) bring institutional knowledge and mentorship. Faculty advisors bring wisdom and accountability. Together, we're not a club—we're a leadership development ecosystem.

Your Leadership Journey Starts Here
What Joining LEADS Means
Joining LEADS isn't just joining a club. You're joining a structured leadership development program. You're committing to learning through doing—running real programs, building your network, and developing the attitudes and skills that define purpose-driven leaders. In return, you get mentorship from industry, access to our partner network, and a community of ambitious peers committed to building something bigger than themselves.
The LEADS Journey
Year 1 – Trainee Associate: You enter as a Trainee Associate, attend all core LEADS programs, help organize events, and learn the ethos of the organization. You're building foundational skills and deciding your area of focus—whether that's innovation, sustainability, mentorship, or community building.
Year 2+ – Core Executive: If you're driving impact, you're invited to become a Core Executive. You lead specific programs or functions (Innovation Challenge, Mentorship Circles, Sustainability Retreat, etc.), mentor incoming Trainee Associates, and take on greater responsibility. This is where you see the full picture of building an organization.
Leadership Roles: From Core Executives emerge student leaders who step into formal roles—Chief Coordinator, Vice-President, General Secretary, President. These roles come with governance responsibilities and direct mentorship from faculty advisors.
What You Gain
Mentorship from Industry – Direct access to senior leaders from McKinsey, WRI, Tata Sustainability Group, and other partners. Monthly mentorship circles, Fireside Talks, and one-on-one guidance as you navigate career decisions.
Skills Development – You'll lead projects, manage teams, think strategically, and learn how to communicate across levels. No classroom can teach what you'll learn building a real program.
Network – 1,500+ contacts through BLS alone. Plus, your peer network—student leaders from every discipline and batch at MSRUAS, and connections to leaders in startups, corporates, and NGOs.
Portfolio – You build a portfolio of real work. When you interview for internships, jobs, or further education, you can talk about the programs you've led, the teams you've built, and the impact you've created.
First-Mover Advantage – LEADS is still relatively young. Early joiners help shape the culture and programs. You're not just participating in LEADS; you're building it.
Open Roles
We typically recruit Trainee Associates in September and January (start of academic years). If you're a second-year or beyond student and passionate about leadership, sustainability, or community building, we also look for mentors and event leads year-round. The best time to join is when you're ready. The best way to apply is to reach out directly—either email leads.msruas@msruas.ac.in or attend a LEADS event to connect with current leaders.
How to Apply
Recruitment Season: LEADS opens applications after summer break (September) and mid-year (January). We announce recruitment through email and social channels—watch for the call.
Informal First: Don't wait for official recruitment. Attend a LEADS event, talk to current members, ask questions. Many of our strongest members came through informal conversations, not formal applications.
What We're Looking For: Curiosity. Ambition. A willingness to step outside your comfort zone. You don't need prior leadership experience—you need the appetite to build it. We're deliberately multi-disciplinary, so we're looking for engineers, designers, artists, scientists, and business students alike.

WHO WE ARE
LEADS Next Gen Centre is the Centre for Leadership Empowering Attitude Development for Sustainability at MSRUAS (Maharaja Srikantadatta Narasimha Raja University of Agricultural Sciences). We are a campus-based leadership development platform dedicated to building the next generation of purpose-driven leaders.
We are not a traditional entrepreneurship club or business fraternity. LEADS is a multi-disciplinary movement welcoming students from engineering, arts, sciences, and commerce. We partner with global organizations—McKinsey, World Resources Institute (WRI), and Tata Sustainability Group—to deliver industry-grade mentorship and real-world learning. Through eight core programs and our national flagship event, Bharat Lead Summit, we equip students with the skills, networks, and mindsets to lead solutions to India's most pressing challenges.
OUR STORY: FROM E-CELL TO LEADS
Three years ago, E-Cell (Entrepreneurship Cell) at MSRUAS was focused primarily on startups and business plan competitions. But something shifted as we worked more closely with students. We realized that the most ambitious students weren't just asking "How do I build a startup?" They were asking deeper questions: How do I be a leader with integrity in a complex world? How do I use innovation to solve sustainability challenges? How do I build teams and movements that create lasting impact?
These questions went beyond entrepreneurship. They pointed to a gap we could fill. So in 2023, E-Cell evolved into LEADS Next Gen Centre. We kept the entrepreneurial spirit—the audacity to imagine and build—but repositioned ourselves as a leadership development platform. We expanded from a faculty club to a multi-disciplinary community. We brought in industry partners like McKinsey and WRI who could add rigor and real-world grounding to everything we teach.
Today, LEADS is a visible force on campus. We run eight programs, host India's first national sustainability-leadership summit, and are recognized by the university as a key pillar of student development. More importantly, our community is growing—and alumni are already leading teams at startups, corporates, and impact organizations.
VISION & MISSION
VISION: To pioneer a global culture of transformative leadership by empowering students to cultivate purpose-driven attitudes, champion sustainable innovation, and lead with integrity.

MISSION: To empower future leaders through experiential learning, ethical mentorship, and sustainability-driven action.
WHAT WE OFFER: EIGHT CORE PROGRAMS
Innovation Challenge – A semester-long competition where students pitch breakthrough ideas addressing social or environmental challenges. Winners receive mentorship, potential funding, and connections to accelerators and investors. It's not just about the pitch; it's about learning to turn ideas into reality.
Transformation Cohorts – Small-group cohorts (8–12 students) working intensively on developing leadership capabilities. Through monthly sessions led by faculty and industry mentors, you explore topics like systems thinking, ethical decision-making, and building influence. It's peer learning combined with guided reflection.
Sustainability Retreat – A multi-day immersive experience (typically 2–3 days) where student leaders explore sustainable innovation with industry practitioners. Past retreats have focused on climate tech, circular economy, and social entrepreneurship. You leave with new frameworks, new peers, and ideas you'll continue exploring.
Fireside Talks – Intimate, unscripted conversations with leaders from our partner organizations and beyond. These aren't formal lectures. They're candid exchanges where leaders share their biggest failures, decisions they regret, and how they think about impact. You'll see leadership as human, not heroic.
Leadership Talk Series – Monthly webinars featuring high-impact leaders discussing contemporary challenges: AI governance, climate finance, building resilient teams, etc. Each talk is followed by Q&A. Recordings are shared with the community so you never miss an opportunity to learn.
Mentorship Circles – Structured mentorship connecting you with senior professionals. You might work 1:1 with a McKinsey consultant, or join a small circle (4–5 students) meeting monthly with a WRI expert. Mentorship is two-way: they guide your thinking; you share youth perspectives that challenge their assumptions.
Corporate Webinars – Direct learning from our partners on industry trends, hiring practices, and skill-building. These sessions are often exclusive to LEADS members, giving you insider knowledge about what companies are actually looking for.
Green Leaders Circle – For students passionate about sustainability, a dedicated community co-creating solutions. This isn't classroom learning. You're building real solutions—from campus sustainability projects to policy briefs to product concepts in green tech. You're doing the work of changemakers.
FLAGSHIP EVENT: BHARAT LEAD SUMMIT (BLS) 2026
Bharat Lead Summit is LEADS's national platform, held annually at MSRUAS. It brings together 1,500+ student leaders, professionals, and changemakers from across India to explore the themes shaping the future.
2026 Theme: Sustainable Leadership & AI – How leaders balance rapid technological change with environmental responsibility. How do we build AI that serves humanity and the planet?
BLS isn't a traditional conference. It's a three-day immersive experience combining keynotes, interactive simulations, expert panels, a green venture showcase, and intensive networking. Delegates work through case studies (build a sustainability strategy, pitch a green venture, navigate an AI ethics dilemma), interact directly with industry leaders, and form the connections that often lead to internships, collaborations, and long-term friendships.
BLS is where India's emerging leaders meet. If you're serious about making an impact, you need to be there.

OUR GOVERNANCE STRUCTURE
LEADS operates under a clear governance model ensuring both academic rigor and student ownership:
Patron: Vice-Chancellor, MSRUAS – Provides institutional vision and ensures alignment with university mission.
Chief Advisor: Dean, Faculty of Management & Commerce – Oversees strategic direction and resource allocation.
Centre Head: Nominated faculty member – Manages day-to-day operations and mentors the student leadership team.
Student Leadership: Composed of President, General Secretary, Vice-President, Chief Coordinator, and extended team of Core Executives and Trainee Associates. Student leaders own the vision and execution.
This structure ensures accountability while keeping decision-making agile and student-centered.
INDUSTRY COLLABORATIONS
McKinsey – Global strategy and leadership consulting firm. McKinsey mentors lead sessions on systems thinking, decision-making under uncertainty, and building influence. They also run case simulations at Bharat Lead Summit.
World Resources Institute (WRI) – Leading research organization focused on sustainability and climate action. WRI experts lead our Sustainability Retreat, mentor the Green Leaders Circle, and contribute to BLS curriculum design.
Tata Sustainability Group – Part of the Tata conglomerate, TSG is building solutions across climate, circular economy, and social impact. Tata leaders mentor LEADS members and judge the Innovation Challenge.
These partnerships are active, not nominal. Our partners don't just lend their name—they contribute curriculum, mentors, and strategic thinking.
STUDENT STRUCTURE & MEMBERSHIP JOURNEY
Trainee Associate (Year 1): Entry-level membership open to all MSRUAS students. Trainees attend all core programs, help organize events, and get to know the LEADS ecosystem. This is your chance to explore and decide how deeply you want to be involved.
Core Executive (Year 2+): If you're driving impact, you're invited to step into a Core Executive role. Core Execs lead specific programs or functions and mentor incoming Trainees. You're now building the organization, not just participating in it.
Leadership Roles: Chief Coordinator, Vice-President, General Secretary, President – These formal roles come with governance responsibilities and direct mentorship from faculty advisors. Leadership roles are determined through peer nomination and faculty guidance.
This progression ensures that LEADS grows from the inside, with students who understand the mission deeply shaping the movement.
